Explain your decisions. Or lose control.

When you cannot explain your decisions, you are already losing.

It starts subtly.

  • You tweak prompts.
  • You refactor quickly.
  • You move branches around.
  • You “just try something.”

The system works.

Until it doesn’t.

Then you realize:

  • You don’t remember the conditions under which that choice was made.
  • You don’t remember the trade-offs you accepted.
  • You don’t remember the risks you ignored.
  • You don’t remember the moment you switched direction.

There is no decision trail. There is no structural memory.

And without that, there is no accountability. No reproducibility. No long-term stability.

RTS is not a logging tool.

It is a decision accountability layer.

It captures reasoning, context, hesitation, conviction, environmental conditions, and switching points — so that when pressure comes, you don’t collapse into guesswork.
