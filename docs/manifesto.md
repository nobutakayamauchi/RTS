# RTS Manifesto

AI systems do not fail because they are wrong.

They fail because authority is not recorded.

When systems break, the same questions appear:

- Who approved this?
- Under what assumptions?
- Who had the authority?
- What was the boundary of responsibility?

The technical failure is rarely the core issue.

The structural absence is.

RTS exists to close that absence.

## The Missing Structure

Modern AI workflows accelerate execution.

But they do not preserve:

- decision authority
- scope of responsibility
- approval boundaries
- state at commitment time

This creates post-failure confusion.

Blame emerges where structure is missing.

## Blame vs Reconstructability

RTS is not a blame system.

It is a reconstructability protocol.

It separates:

- Human decision ownership  
from  
- System behavioral outcome  

By logging decision boundaries, RTS prevents:

- authority drift
- assumption collapse
- responsibility ambiguity

## The Core Insight

Different industries.
Different failures.
Same structural gap.

The missing piece is always the same:

Decision authority is not anchored.

RTS anchors it.

## Structural Governance for AI

RTS introduces:

- deterministic execution logs
- state transition markers
- decision boundary events

Governance is not compliance.

It is structural clarity.

## Why This Matters

AI does not reduce human responsibility.

It amplifies it.

Without structural authority logging,
scale becomes fragility.

RTS restores structural memory.

Not correctness.

Continuity.
