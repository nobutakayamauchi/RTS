Execution Provenance as Infrastructure

Artificial intelligence accelerates execution.
It does not preserve decision context.

Modern systems scale output,
but they do not scale accountability.

RTS formalizes execution provenance as an operational layer.

Definition

RTS (Recording & Transparency System) is a structured logging protocol that preserves:

  • decision intent
  • operational assumptions
  • environmental constraints
  • outcome evaluation

RTS treats execution history as first-class infrastructure.

Problem Statement

AI workflows generate outputs without retaining:

  • why a decision was made
  • which assumptions were active
  • which alternatives were rejected
  • what environmental conditions influenced action

Speed increases. Traceability collapses.

Conceptual Position

RTS is not:

  • memory embedding
  • vector retrieval
  • workflow automation
  • monitoring software

RTS is execution provenance formalization.

Industrial Application

  • AI-assisted decision environments
  • high-accountability workflows
  • research systems
  • regulated industries
  • organizational audit trails

RTS is tool-agnostic. It operates above implementation layers.

Governance Model

RTS is open and public.
The protocol is independent from professional services applying it.

RTS is infrastructure. Application is specialization.

Structural Continuity

Execution without provenance is operational fragility.

RTS introduces structural continuity.
